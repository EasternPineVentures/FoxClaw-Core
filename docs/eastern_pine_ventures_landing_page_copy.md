## Product Signal

### CoinFox

CoinFox is the public product: a receipt-first trading social platform for posts, comments,
votes, watchlists, public market reads, paper-first practice, and outcome review.

It is designed for market learners and active retail traders who already follow trading
communities, but want better structure, accountability, and review before risking real
money.

### FoxClaw

FoxClaw is the internal intelligence backbone. It helps organize market claims, evidence,
risk, outcomes, and learning receipts while keeping private system mechanics protected.

FoxClaw is not the public social network and it is not a trading bot. It is the protected
R&D layer that helps make CoinFox more accountable over time.

### Planifier

Planifier is the planning and practice layer. It helps turn public intelligence into
checklists, routines, journals, and personal decision discipline.

### Redshift

Redshift is the protected research lane for experiments, simulations, and higher-risk ideas
that need proof before they become product promises.

## Problem Section

### Headline

Market discussion moves fast. Memory does not.

### Body

Trading communities generate a constant stream of calls, opinions, screenshots, reactions,
questions, and trade ideas. Some of it is useful. Much of it is noisy. Almost none of it is
consistently structured, followed up on, or measured.

That makes it hard to know who added value, what risks were missed, what ideas worked, and
what should be remembered.

CoinFox is built for that gap.

## How It Works

```text
Users post market ideas
        ->
Ideas become structured claims
        ->
Evidence, risk, and context are attached
        ->
Discussion and votes raise review priority
        ->
Outcomes are tracked
        ->
The system learns what was useful
```

## Safety Positioning

CoinFox is paper-first and education-first. It is not a brokerage, not a copy-trading
system, not a paid signal room, and not personalized financial advice.

Votes, comments, and attention can show where people are looking. They cannot decide what is
true. Evidence and outcomes still matter.

The next step is turning that foundation into a polished public prototype, early beta flow,
